Discussing Black maternal health solutions on Capitol Hill

Black women, no matter their education level or income bracket, are three times more likely to face life-threatening pregnancy complications than White women. On Apr‍il 2‍9, Cristal Gary, Meridian Plan President and CEO, participated in a panel discussion during the 2024 Annual NMQF Leadership Summit in Washington, D.C. Alongside fellow experts, she addressed the Black maternal health crisis and approaches to affect change and scale solutions for health equity.

Help us move the needle on maternal health outcomes

Did you know that members who complete a Notification of Pregnancy (NOP) are three times more likely to be compliant with prenatal care? And they can earn up to $250 and free baby gear through our expanded Healthy Rewards program.

Encourage patients to submit their NOP early via the member portal, or you can complete it on their behalf through the secure provider portal. A completed NOP will connect your patients to Meridian’s care management team and high-risk members to Start Smart for Your Baby® — our multi-faceted program that includes enhanced outreach, wellness materials, intensive care management, and provider collaboration to lower the risks of premature births and admissions to neonatal intensive care units.

New 20‍24 P4P incentive: Capturing new and expectant moms’ health‑related social needs

We’re proud to unveil a new P4P incentive that rewards providers an extra $10 for including Z Codes on claims for prenatal and postpartum visits. Learn more about Z Codes, which capture patients’ social conditions such as education, housing, employment, and food access. By systematically documenting Z Code information, together we can develop interventions that take a whole-person approach to healthcare.

Celebrate Women’s Health Month as a champion for cancer screening

• Providers can earn incentives for delivering these preventive services through our enhanced 20‍24 P4P program.
• Promote breast cancer screening by sharing our Fast Facts for Better Health video with your patients and networks.

Long COVID and depression

Studies emerging about Long COVID offer providers valuable insights, such as findings that depression is the most prominent symptom associated with the condition. Our recent provider notice outlines the risk factors for developing Long COVID, the most common symptoms, and guidance for treating mental health conditions associated with it, including the antidepressant medications on the Meridian Preferred Drug List.

Updated YouthCare Provider Manual released

YouthCare pharmacy update: Psychotropic medications

Effective Jun‍e 1, 20‍24, the status of the medications buprenorphine, buprenorphine-naloxone, methadone, naltrexone, and naltrexone-triamcinolone will change for YouthCare members under the care of the Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS). The drugs will require the form CFS 431-A (also known as a consent form) to be submitted to DCFS.
