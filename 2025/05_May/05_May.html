Mom’s the word: Advancing programs to support maternal health

Moms and moms‑to‑be represent an important Meridian population. Here’s how we’re stepping up to improve their health:
• Through a new partnership with the March of Dimes and Advocate Health, we’re supporting virtual and in-person community events to promote healthy pregnancies. Stay tuned for more details about this unique collaboration.
• View our first webinar held during Black Maternal Health Week. Speakers discussed solutions to improve outcomes for moms of color.
• Our Start Smart for Your Baby® maternity program aims to connect pregnant members to personalized resources: Learn how to easily refer your patient.
• The 20‍25 Pay for Performance program features higher incentives for timely prenatal and postpartum care. See our HEDIS® Quick Reference Guide (page 119) for care gap closure information.

QI Insight: Refresher on women’s health HEDIS measures

It’s National Women’s Health Week. Take a minute to review three essential women’s health HEDIS measures in our 20‍25 Pay for Performance program.
• Breast Cancer Screening (BCS-E)
• Cervical Cancer Screening (CCS-E)
• Chlamydia Screening in Women (CHL)

Find details and billing guidance about these measures in the “Women’s Health” section of the HEDIS Quick Reference Guide.

Access no-cost continuing education

As a Meridian provider, you have unlimited access to dozens of accredited continuing education courses through the Centene Institute for Advanced Education™—all at no cost. Our growing catalog of evidence-based courses supports ongoing professional development and maintenance of clinical licensure.

Behavioral health providers also have access to specialized training courses on mental health first aid, adverse childhood experiences, and other timely clinical topics. Find details on our Provider Education page.

Coming soon: Your annual Provider Satisfaction Survey

Feedback from providers through the Provider Satisfaction Survey is the foundation of our health plan’s improvement initiatives. Please take a moment to thoughtfully complete your survey when you receive it in the mail.

Health Benefits for Immigrant Adults (HBIA) coverage ends Ju‍ne 3‍0, 20‍25

Medical coverage for Illinois Medicaid members enrolled in the HBIA program—which covers adults ages 42 to 64—will end Ju‍ne 3‍0, 20‍25, at 11:59 p.m. The Health Benefits for Immigrant Seniors program will continue to serve enrolled members ages 65 and over. Review our provider notice for guidance about the transition.

Notable HFS notices
• New guidance was released regarding the Initiation of Medication Assisted Treatment for Opioid Use Disorder in hospital emergency departments.
• Effective May 1, 2025, providers billing for Mobile Crisis Response services are required to transition to the updated Illinois Medicaid Crisis Assessment Tool (IM-CAT) tool.
• HFS has expanded the Handicapping Labio-Lingual Deviation (HLD) orthodontic scoring tool to include additional automatic qualifiers and a new reporting requirement on denials.
