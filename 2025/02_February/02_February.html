Health Happens Here Grants™

Organizations across Illinois are closing care gaps and addressing health-related social needs of Meridian members and their local communities. Through our Health Happens Here Grants, we’re proud to invest in this work—from food pantry resources in Murphysboro to nursing career pathways in Belleville.

We closed out 20‍24 by awarding nearly $1.4 million to 25 organizations. “These grants will support behavioral health and wellness programs, support for young mothers, professional and career development, mobile health units, and non‑emergency transport services, all of which are critical to the health of these communities,” said Cristal Gary, Meridian Plan President and CEO.

Earn dollars for your practice with our Pay for Performance (P4P) program

Through our P4P program, contracted providers can earn incentives for delivering quality preventive care. In response to your feedback, we’ve enhanced our 20‍25 P4P incentives. Providers can now earn more rewards for serving Meridian and YouthCare members.
• An additional 50th percentile target expands incentives
• Rewards are available to providers like OB/GYNs and behavioral health clinicians for two key measures
• Increased dollar amounts for areas such as childhood immunizations and maternal healthcare recognize exceptional care

Help members take heart health to heart

During American Heart Month this February, let’s join forces to promote prevention and management of cardiovascular disease (CVD). Here are resources to support you and your patients:
• Guide patients to our HealthSheets™ library, with resources in English and Spanish to help them understand heart disease risk factors, cholesterol tests, and high blood pressure.
• Remind your Meridian patients to sign up for our Healthy Rewards program. They can earn prepaid debit cards for completing their annual checkup, screenings, and more.
• Refer patients with CVD to Meridian’s Chronic Disease Management Program. Eligible members can receive health coaching and resources like a home blood pressure monitor at no cost.
• Recommend specialized programs for patients with CVD risk factors, such as an accredited Diabetes Prevention Program covered by Medicaid.

Pharmacy spotlight: 20‍25 American Diabetes Association (ADA) updates

The ADA has released its annual Standards of Care, which aim to improve outcomes for children and adults with diabetes. The 2025 recommendations have been updated to further include people with a high risk of developing diabetes. Notable changes include:
• Guidance on the use of GLP‑1 receptor agonists beyond weight loss
• Considerations related to continuous glucose monitors and other technology
• Potentially harmful medications during pregnancy

There’s still time to prevent RSV

The risk of acute respiratory illness causing people to seek care remains serious in Illinois, with IDPH tracking the burden of these diseases. Unlike the flu or COVID-19, infants and young children represent the largest age group admitted to hospitals for respiratory syncytial virus (RSV). View our updated RSV provider notice for options available to patients.

Stay enrolled as a Medicaid provider: Know your date to revalidate

Revalidation every five years is a federal requirement for Medicaid providers that was paused during COVID‑19. Illinois resumed this process in Sept‍ember 20‍24. Providers who did not revalidate in 2024 should prepare to take this step in 2025—or risk being terminated from Medicaid without the ability to receive payment.
• Do you know your organization’s current revalidation status?
• Do you know when your 90-day revalidation cycle will begin?
• Are you certain your organization’s contact information in the IMPACT system is accurate?

If you answered “no” to any of these questions, review our recent provider notice for resources.
