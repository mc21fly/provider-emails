Celebrating National Doctors’ Day

March 30 is National Doctors’ Day, and we’re celebrating the thousands of physicians who champion the health of our members. Thank you to each and every one of them.

“We are grateful to all physicians who answer the call to heal others,” says Meridian Chief Medical Officer Rutu Ezhuthachan, MD. “But we are especially thankful for those who serve Medicaid patients, people who are often the most vulnerable in our communities and whose lives are transformed because of your skilled, compassionate care."

Curbing colorectal cancer

Colorectal cancer is the third-most common cancer and its incidence in adults under age 50 is rising globally. During Colorectal Cancer Awareness Month, join Meridian in promoting preventive screening with resources for you and your patients:
• Meridian members over age 45 can earn a prepaid $50 debit card through our Healthy Rewards program after completing their colorectal cancer screening.
• Use our HealthSheets™ library, with resources in English and Spanish about how to prepare for a colonoscopy, colorectal cancer risk factors, and prevention strategies.
• Watch and share our Fast Facts for Better Health video about colorectal cancer warning signs and screening.

Watch: How care coordination is helping Lucas thrive

After turning 18, Lucas found it difficult to maintain healthcare for his disability. That’s when Catharine, a Meridian care manager, stepped in and connected him to care that “made everything better,” according to his mother, Nanci.

Reducing hypertension with healthy foods

Meridian’s Food is Medicine pilot program, in partnership with the Greater Chicago Food Depository, is a new way we’re supporting members with high blood pressure. A cohort of members who live in south Chicago will be selected for the pilot program and receive heart-healthy meals and food each week. They will also be connected to:
• Education from a registered dietitian on grocery shopping and meal prep
• One-on-one care management support
• Continued blood pressure monitoring

In case you missed it
• Meridian providers can now use Availity to verify member eligibility, submit and check claims, manage authorizations and submit medical record documentation to close HEDIS® care gaps.
• The Department of Healthcare and Family Services (HFS) released a new handbook for Community‑Based Behavioral Services providers.
• Has your organization completed revalidation with HFS? If not, avoid being disenrolled from the Medicaid program by reviewing our provider notice and ensuring your contact information is accurate in IMPACT.
